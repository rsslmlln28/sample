Computers come in different types. There are small in size and there are also huge ones. However, they are generally categorized by their speed and computing power, and their names usually entail their size and capability. Now, lets find out the different types of computer.

 

 

 

Types of Computer

 

 

 

 

Microcomputer

 

 

 

 

A microcomputer is a small, relatively inexpensive computer designed for a single user. It uses a microprocessor as its CPU, and integrates a memory and minimal input/output circuit on a single printed circuit board.

 

There are two classifications of microcomputers: Personal Computer (PC) and Portable Computer.

 

 

 

Personal Computer (PC)

 

 

 

 

 

 

A personal computer, or also called a desktop computer, is a multifunctional computer that is widely welcomed because of its low price and ease of use.

 

 

 

Portable Computer

 

 

 

 

 

 

A portable computer is a computer that can be easily moved from one place to another. It comes with its own display and keyboard. Examples of portable computers include: laptops, tablets, and smartphones.

 

 

 

 

Workstation

 

 

 

 

 

 

A workstation PC is a computer designed for specialized tasks. Workstations usually have dedicated modules, which are different from mainstream computers, that can perform heavy computing or graphics tasks.

 

 

 

 

Minicomputer

 

 

 

The first w:Data General Nova minicomputer, from 1969, at the Computer History Museum
ArnoldReinhold, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

The minicomputer is placed in between the workstation and the mainframe computer when it comes to size and power. Generally, a minicomputer is a multi-processing system that can accommodate upto 250 users at the same time.

 

 

 

 

Mainframe

 

 

 

A pair of IBM mainframes. On the left is the IBM z Systems z13. On the right is the IBM LinuxONE Rockhopper.
Agiorgio, CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

A mainframe computer is quite big and very costly in nature. It is able to support hundreds or even thousands of concurrent users. At the same time, mainframe computers can run multiple programs and facilitate many simultaneous program executions.

 

 

 

 

Supercomputer

 

 

 

PRIMEHPC FX1000 (Fugaku node) at SC19.

By Raysonho @ Open Grid Scheduler / Scalable Grid Engine, CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons

 

 

 

So far, supercomputers are the fastest type of computer available. Supercomputers are very costly and are utilized for special uses involving enormous amounts of mathematical calculations.
